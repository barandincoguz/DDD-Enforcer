Hospital Management Information System (HMIS)
1. Introduction

This Software Requirements Specification (SRS) defines the requirements for a Hospital Management Information System (HMIS). The system aims to digitize and integrate hospital operations including patient management, clinical workflows, billing, and reporting.

2. Purpose

The purpose of this document is to provide a comprehensive description of system behavior, constraints, and requirements for developers, healthcare administrators, medical staff, and regulatory bodies.

3. Scope

The HMIS will support patient registration, appointment scheduling, electronic medical records (EMR), laboratory management, pharmacy management, billing, and reporting.

4. Definitions and Acronyms

EMR: Electronic Medical Record

HMIS: Hospital Management Information System

5. Overall Description
5.1 Product Perspective

The HMIS is a centralized, web-based system that replaces manual and fragmented hospital processes.

5.2 User Classes

Patients

Doctors

Nurses

Administrative Staff

System Administrators

5.3 Operating Environment

Web browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Edge)

Secure hospital network and cloud servers

6. Functional Requirements
6.1 Patient Management

The system shall register new patients with unique identifiers.

The system shall store patient demographics and medical history.

6.2 Appointment Scheduling

Patients shall request appointments online.

Doctors shall manage their availability schedules.

6.3 Electronic Medical Records

Doctors shall create and update EMRs.

EMRs shall include diagnoses, prescriptions, and test results.

6.4 Laboratory Module

Lab staff shall record test results electronically.

Doctors shall view lab results in patient records.

6.5 Pharmacy Module

Pharmacists shall manage medication inventory.

Prescriptions shall be digitally issued and tracked.

6.6 Billing and Payments

The system shall generate invoices automatically.

The system shall support insurance and self-pay billing.

7. Non-Functional Requirements
7.1 Performance

The system shall support 5,000 concurrent users.

Response time shall not exceed 2 seconds for critical operations.

7.2 Security

Role-based access control shall be enforced.

All sensitive data shall be encrypted at rest and in transit.

7.3 Reliability and Availability

System uptime shall be at least 99.9%.

Automated backups shall be performed daily.

7.4 Compliance

The system shall comply with healthcare data regulations (e.g., HIPAA-like standards).

8. Constraints

Integration with existing hospital hardware systems.

Regulatory approval requirements.

9. Future Enhancements

AI-assisted diagnostics

Telemedicine integration

Mobile patient applications
